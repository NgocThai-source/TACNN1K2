What is Recyclable Waste?

These are types of waste that can be recycled into new materials or products, including:

✅ Should go in recycling bin

  • Plastic bottles (PET, HDPE) cleaned
  • Aluminum and metal cans (soda, beer)
  • Office paper, newspapers, magazines
  • Cardboard (flattened)
  • Glass bottles and jars (unbroken)
  • Paper milk cartons (rinsed)

❌ Should not go in

  • Plastic without recycling symbol
  • Food-soiled paper (dirty pizza boxes)
  • Paper cups with plastic lining
  • Mirrors, window glass
  • Multi-layer packaging (noodle packets, snacks)

Common Recycling Symbols

PET (number 1)
Water bottles, soft drinks
HDPE (number 2)
Milk jugs, shampoo bottles
Correct Handling
  1. Clean thoroughly (for food containers)
  2. Remove components (bottle caps, labels)
  3. Flatten to save space
  4. Sort by material if possible
  5. Place in recycling bin or take to collection point
Important note:

Plastic bottles should be capped when recycled to prevent them from being mixed with regular waste.

What is Organic Waste?

These are types of waste that can be composted:

✅ Should go in organic bin

  • Leftover food (vegetables, meat, rice)
  • Fruit and vegetable peels
  • Coffee grounds, tea leaves
  • Leafy greens, dry grass
  • Rotten fruits

❌ Should not go in

  • Large animal bones
  • Used cooking oil
  • Shells, hard shells
  • Nylon bags, plastic packaging
Home Composting Guide
  1. Prepare compost bin with lid
  2. Separate organic waste from other waste
  3. Cut organic waste into small pieces (3-5cm) for quick decomposition
  4. Mix with wood chips or dry leaves in a 2:1 ratio
  5. Turn 2-3 times/week
  6. After 2-3 months, you can use compost
Tip:

Add ground eggshells to compost for calcium supplement for plants.

What is Hazardous Waste?

These are types of waste that contain harmful chemicals or pose a risk to human health and the environment:

✅ Should go in hazardous waste bin

  • Batteries, chargers
  • Fluorescent and LED bulbs, broken
  • Expired medicines
  • Paints, chemical solvents
  • Strong cleaning agents, weed killers

❌ Should not go in

  • Regular household waste
  • Recyclable waste
  • Organic waste
Correct Handling
  1. Ensure batteries and bulbs are not broken before disposal
  2. Take to designated hazardous waste collection points
  3. Do not mix harmful substances with regular waste to prevent contamination

What is Special Handling Waste?

These are types of waste that do not belong to recyclable, organic, or hazardous waste categories, but need separate handling due to difficulty in recycling or potential impact on the environment if handled incorrectly, for example:

✅ Should go in special handling waste bin

  • Household items, broken, cannot be repaired
  • Nylon bags, cannot be recycled
  • Used toothbrush, cannot be repaired
  • Broken toys
  • Non-recyclable packaging (bubble wrap, fleece)
  • Food packaging with oil, foam

❌ Should not go in

  • Recyclable waste
  • Organic waste
  • Hazardous waste
Correct Handling
  1. Separate special handling waste and place in separate bin (yellow)
  2. Ensure non-recyclable items are sealed to prevent leakage or spreading to the environment
  3. Do not mix with recyclable, organic, or hazardous waste

Advanced Sorting Tips

Reduce Waste from Source

  • Use cloth bags when shopping
  • Choose products with minimal packaging
  • Buy in bulk to reduce packaging
  • Prioritize reusable products

Waste Handling for Food

  • Plan meals to reduce leftover food
  • Store food properly
  • Utilize leftover food for new ingredients
  • Freeze food nearing expiration
